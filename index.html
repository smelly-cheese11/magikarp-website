What is a Pokémon?

Pokémon is a fictional species of creatures that appear in a variety of media. The term "Pokémon" is short for "Pocket Monsters," the original Japanese name for the franchise. In the Pokémon franchise, humans, called Pokémon Trainers, catch, train, and battle Pokémon for sport. The main goal for many Trainers is to become a Pokémon Master by capturing and training a diverse team of Pokémon and defeating other Trainers in battles. Pokémon can be found in video games, trading card games, TV shows, movies, and other forms of media. The franchise takes place in a shared universe in which humans co-exist with creatures known as Pokémon, a large variety of species endowed with special powers.

Here are some facts about Pokémon: Pokémon started as a video game in 1996. There are over 1,000 species of Pokémon. Designs for the multitude of species can draw inspiration from anything such as animals, plants and mythological creatures. The original Pokémon games are Japanese role-playing video games (RPGs) with an element of strategy. Poké Balls catch and store Pokémon.
